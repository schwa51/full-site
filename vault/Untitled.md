## Mindful breathing

| Card Title | Best For | Time | Steps | Focus Cue | Gentle Note | Icon Suggestion |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Basic breath focus | Starting the day; settling down | 2–5 min | Sit comfortably. Notice the air at your nose, chest, or belly. When your mind wanders, gently return to the breath. | Come back to one breath. | Wandering is normal. | Lungs or soft breath wave.[1][5] |
| Box breathing | Stress; tension; feeling keyed up | 1–3 min | Inhale for 4. Hold for 4. Exhale for 4. Hold for 4. Repeat slowly. | Slow and steady. | Skip the holds if they feel uncomfortable. | Square outline with directional arrows.[5] |
| In / out labeling | Busy mind; simple focus | 1–3 min | Breathe naturally. Silently say “in” on the inhale and “out” on the exhale. Keep following the next breath. | Label and return. | Use a soft mental voice. | Inward and outward arrows around a circle.[1][5] |
| Color breathing | Emotional overload; calming down | 2–4 min | Imagine breathing in a calming color and breathing out a stressful color. Keep the image simple and repeat. | Breathe in calm, out tension. | The colors can be any colors that feel helpful. | Breath icon with a subtle gradient.[5] |
| Pause-focused breathing | Slowing down; centering | 1–3 min | Notice the inhale, the exhale, and the tiny pause between them. Rest your attention in those small pauses. | Notice the spaces between breaths. | Do not force the pause. | Pause symbol inside a circle.[5] |

## Observe and Describe

| Card Title | Best For | Time | Steps | Focus Cue | Gentle Note | Icon Suggestion |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Single-object observation | Focus practice; grounding | 2–5 min | Pick one object. Study its color, shape, texture, and details. Stay with it without changing anything. | Look closely. | Curiosity works better than effort. | Magnifying glass.[7][5] |
| Describe the room | Feeling scattered; getting present | 2–5 min | Look around and name what you see in plain, factual words. Stick to colors, shapes, light, and position. | Describe, don’t evaluate. | Replace “ugly” with concrete details. | Room outline or simple interior frame.[2][5] |
| Internal vs external check | Emotional confusion; self-awareness | 2–4 min | Notice what is inside you, like thoughts, feelings, or body sensations. Then notice what is outside you, like sounds, light, and objects. | What is inside? What is outside? | You do not need to fix anything. | Person silhouette with inner circle and outer ring.[1][5] |
| Thought labeling | Rumination; anxious thinking | 1–3 min | Notice a thought and name it gently: “planning,” “worrying,” or “remembering.” Let it be there and return to the present. | Name the thought, then return. | You are noticing thoughts, not obeying them. | Speech bubble with a small tag.[8][5] |
| Emotion describing | Strong feelings; emotional clarity | 2–4 min | Name the emotion in simple words. Add where you feel it in your body and what it feels like. | Name what you feel. | Keep it factual and brief. | Heart with a pencil or speech bubble.[8][5] |

## One-mindfully in daily life

| Card Title | Best For | Time | Steps | Focus Cue | Gentle Note | Icon Suggestion |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Mindful coffee or tea making | Morning routine; gentle focus | 3–5 min | Make your drink slowly. Notice sound, smell, warmth, and each step in order. | Just make the drink. | Let the routine be the practice. | Mug or teacup with steam.[3][5] |
| Mindful dishwashing | Chores; stress relief | 3–10 min | Wash dishes while noticing water temperature, soap, texture, and hand movements. Bring attention back each time it drifts. | One dish at a time. | No multitasking. | Plate with bubbles or sparkle.[3][5] |
| Mindful tooth-brushing | Habit stacking; short daily practice | 2 min | Feel the brush, taste the toothpaste, and notice the brushing motion. Stay with the task until it is done. | Brush and notice. | A tiny practice still counts. | Toothbrush icon.[3][5] |
| Mindful shower | Reset after stress; evening wind-down | 3–10 min | Notice water temperature, sound, scent, and how the water feels on your skin. Return to the sensations when your mind wanders. | Feel the water. | Keep attention on direct experience. | Showerhead with water drops.[5] |
| Mindful walking | Breaks; transitions; nervous energy | 2–10 min | Walk at a natural pace. Notice each footstep, shifting weight, and the rhythm of movement. | Step by step. | Slow down only if it helps. | Footsteps or walking figure.[7][5] |
| Mindful phone check | Impulses; distraction; digital habits | 30–60 sec | Pause before unlocking your phone. Notice the urge, take one breath, and choose whether to continue. | Pause before tapping. | The goal is awareness, not perfection. | Smartphone with pause symbol.[3][5] |

## Sensory grounding

| Card Title | Best For | Time | Steps | Focus Cue | Gentle Note | Icon Suggestion |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 5-4-3-2-1 sense scan | Anxiety; panic; overwhelm | 2–5 min | Name 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste. Move slowly through each sense. | Use your senses to come back now. | If one sense is hard, substitute a memory of it. | Five-dot pattern or five-senses cluster.[2][5] |
| Room describing | Dissociation; feeling unreal | 2–5 min | Look around and describe the room in concrete detail. Focus on shape, color, size, light, and location. | Stay with what is here. | Keep your descriptions simple. | House or room outline with an eye.[2][5] |
| Texture hunt | Restlessness; racing thoughts | 1–3 min | Find three different textures and touch each one slowly. Notice rough, smooth, soft, cool, or firm sensations. | Feel the texture fully. | Compare textures without judging them. | Hand touching patterned surface.[2][5] |
| Temperature reset | High anxiety; agitation | 1–2 min | Hold something cool or warm, or run water over your hands. Focus fully on the temperature and how your body responds. | Notice warm or cool. | Keep it comfortable, not extreme. | Thermometer or sun/snowflake symbol.[2][5] |
| Sound scan | Mental noise; overwhelm | 1–3 min | Listen for near, medium, and far sounds. Name each one without telling a story about it. | Listen outward. | Include quiet sounds too. | Ear or sound wave icon.[2][5] |
| Smell focus | Quick grounding; memory anchor | 1–2 min | Notice one smell around you, or imagine a familiar calming scent in detail. Stay with the scent for a few breaths. | Follow the scent. | Even a faint smell works. | Nose icon or scent curls.[2][5] |

## Body grounding

| Card Title | Best For | Time | Steps | Focus Cue | Gentle Note | Icon Suggestion |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Short body scan | Evening calm; body awareness | 3–10 min | Move your attention slowly through your body from feet to head. Notice sensations without trying to change them. | Scan and notice. | Numbness counts as a sensation too. | Human outline with vertical dotted path.[1][5] |
| Progressive muscle relaxation | Tension; stress; bedtime | 3–10 min | Gently tense one muscle group, then release it. Move through the body and notice the difference between tight and relaxed. | Tighten, release, notice. | Keep the tension gentle. | Flexed arm or segmented body icon.[5] |
| Feet-on-floor grounding | Fast reset; strong emotions | 30–90 sec | Press both feet into the floor. Notice pressure, support, and where your body makes contact with the chair or ground. | Feel the support under you. | Great for use at a desk. | Feet on a ground line.[2][5] |
| Hand grounding | Portable grounding; discreet use | 1–2 min | Hold one hand with the other, or hold a small object. Notice warmth, weight, texture, and pressure. | Feel what is in your hands. | Small sensations are enough. | Two hands together or hand holding a circle.[7][5] |
| Heartbeat exercise | Reconnecting with the body | 1–3 min | Walk in place or move a little, then pause and feel your heartbeat. Rest your attention there for several breaths. | Notice your pulse. | Stop if movement feels uncomfortable. | Heart with ECG line.[5] |

## DBT mini-skills

| Card Title | Best For | Time | Steps | Focus Cue | Gentle Note | Icon Suggestion |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| STOP | Emotional spikes; impulsive moments | 30–60 sec | Stop. Take a breath. Observe what is happening inside and around you. Proceed with one effective next step. | Pause before acting. | Even one breath can help. | Stop sign or raised hand.[9][5] |
| Wise Mind breathing | Decisions; emotional conflict | 2–4 min | Breathe and imagine balancing emotion and reason. Ask yourself what your wisest next step is. | Find the wise next move. | Look for balanced, workable action. | Heart and brain combined icon.[1][5] |
| Observe-only practice | Overthinking; mental clutter | 1–3 min | Notice sensations, sounds, or sights without putting them into words. Let experience come and go. | Notice without narrating. | Wordless awareness may feel awkward at first. | Open eye icon.[8][5] |
| Participate activity | Low mood; disengagement | 3–10 min | Choose one simple activity and do it fully, such as singing, coloring, petting a dog, or tidying. Give it your whole attention. | Throw yourself into the moment. | Choose something small and doable. | Person in motion or raised hand.[4][5] |
| Non-judgmental language drill | Self-criticism; shame spirals | 1–3 min | Take one judgmental thought and rewrite it as a plain fact. Practice describing what happened without labels like good or bad. | Facts, not verdicts. | This is a skill, not forced positivity. | Balanced scales or speech bubble with checkmark.[3][5] |

| Card Title | Best For | Time | Steps | Focus Cue | Gentle Note | Icon Suggestion |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
