- **Genre:** Traditional fantasy adventure
- **Complexity:** Light-to-medium
- **Character growth:** Skill improvement and new abilities; no levels
- **General feel:** Familiar fantasy, fast rules, dangerous combat, and occasional absurdity

## What kind of game is it?

Dragonbane is a traditional fantasy adventure game built around exploration, quests, monsters, treasure, and dangerous combat. Its world is recognizable enough to begin playing without extensive setting knowledge, while still making room for unusual elements such as mallard adventurers, frog people, demons, insect-folk, and stranger monsters.

It occupies a middle ground between modern D&D-style fantasy and rules-light old-school games. Characters have professions, skills, equipment, and special abilities, but the system avoids long lists of combat powers and extensive numerical modifiers.

## Core system

Most actions use a d20 roll-under system: roll equal to or below the relevant skill to succeed. A boon means rolling two dice and keeping the lower result; a bane means keeping the higher. A natural 1 is an exceptional success called a Dragon, while a natural 20 is a major failure called a Demon.

The basic resolution system is straightforward. Most situational advantages and disadvantages are handled through boons and banes rather than by adding modifiers.

Characters also have Willpower Points, which are spent to activate heroic abilities, innate kin abilities, and magic. This adds a resource-management element without creating a complicated action economy.

## Characters

A character combines a kin, profession, attributes, skills, equipment, and one or more heroic abilities. Professions include familiar options such as fighter, hunter, mage, scholar, merchant, and thief.

Professions determine a character’s starting capabilities but do not function as permanent classes. Characters may improve any skill during play and can gradually develop beyond their original profession.

Martial and skill-based professions are relatively simple to operate. Mages have additional rules involving Willpower, spells, magical schools, and spell ranks.

## Combat

Combat is fast but includes meaningful tactical decisions. Initiative cards are drawn each round, so the order can change throughout a fight. Characters normally move and perform one action. Dodging and parrying use that action, requiring players to choose between attacking and preserving the ability to defend themselves.

Armor reduces incoming damage rather than making a character harder to hit. Weapon properties, shields, positioning, conditions, initiative order, and Willpower abilities can all affect a fight, but combat does not depend on extensive grids of powers or modifiers.

Ordinary humanoid opponents use skills and statistics similar to player characters. Larger monsters work differently: many act several times per round and use random attack tables containing distinctive attacks and effects. This gives major creatures their own mechanical identity rather than simply increasing their hit points.

Combat is dangerous. Characters have relatively few hit points, and a strong attack can incapacitate someone quickly. Avoiding, negotiating, or preparing for a fight can be as important as winning one directly.

## Character progression

Dragonbane does not use character levels. During play and at the end of a session, characters may receive advancement marks beside skills they used. Each mark allows an improvement roll; rolling above the current skill value increases that skill by one point.

Because of this system, lower skills are easier to improve than skills that are already high. Advancement is frequent and concrete, but usually occurs in small increments.

Characters can also gain new heroic abilities by reaching high skill levels or completing significant heroic deeds. These abilities generally provide more noticeable new options than ordinary skill increases.

Players who enjoy gradual, flexible improvement may prefer this approach. Players who prefer major level-ups or large increases in power may find progression comparatively understated.

## Exploration and journeys

Travel is divided into six-hour shifts. The rules cover distance, forced marches, navigation, food, weather, camping, sleep, hunting, fishing, and foraging.

The group appoints a pathfinder who makes the main navigation roll for each travel shift. Other characters may contribute through gathering supplies, preparing camp, keeping watch, responding to mishaps, and dealing with encounters.

The procedures provide concrete consequences for poor preparation or failed rolls, but wilderness travel can involve substantial attention to food, time, and equipment. How prominent these rules become will depend on the campaign and the GM.

## Distinctive features

- Professions influence starting abilities without permanently limiting later development.
- Initiative is redrawn each round and can be exchanged between participants.
- Defending requires spending the same action that could otherwise be used to attack.
- Large monsters use randomized attack tables and may act multiple times per round.
- Heroic abilities, kin abilities, and spells all draw from the same Willpower resource.
- The game supports both conventional fantasy characters and more unusual playable kin.

## Practical considerations

Dragonbane is relatively easy to learn while still providing more mechanical structure than a minimalist game. It offers recognizable fantasy archetypes, statted enemies, dangerous combat, and steady character improvement.

Its main limitations are that character advancement is incremental rather than level-based, character-building options are less extensive than in highly tactical fantasy games, and the travel procedures can place the main navigation roll in the hands of one designated character.

Overall: Dragonbane emphasizes accessible rules, quick tactical combat, flexible skill development, and dangerous fantasy adventure. It is best suited to a group that wants more structure than a narrative-first game without committing to the complexity of a heavily optimized d20 system.
