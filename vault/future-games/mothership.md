## What kind of game is it?

Mothership is a science-fiction horror game about crews operating in dangerous places: derelict ships, isolated stations, research facilities, colonies, and unexplored worlds.

Characters may be workers, scientists, soldiers, or androids, but they are not conventional action heroes. The game assumes that space is hostile, employers are unreliable, equipment is limited, and some threats cannot be defeated through direct combat.

A typical scenario combines three pressures: something to survive, something to solve, and someone to save. The central question is usually not simply whether the crew can kill the threat, but what they are willing to risk in order to understand it or escape.

## Core system

Mothership uses percentile dice.

Characters have four Stats:

- Strength
- Speed
- Intellect
- Combat

They also have three Saves:

- Sanity
- Fear
- Body

To succeed, roll d100 below the relevant Stat or Save. Skills add bonuses to the Stat before the roll. Advantage and disadvantage mean rolling twice and keeping the better or worse result.

Doubles produce critical results. A successful double is a critical success; a failed double is a critical failure and usually triggers a Panic Check.

Failed checks and saves often generate Stress, so repeated pressure makes later Panic increasingly likely.

## Characters

There are four main classes:

- **Marine:** Better in combat and more resistant to physical and psychological danger.
- **Android:** Highly intelligent and resistant to Fear, but unsettling to nearby humans.
- **Scientist:** Strongly specialized in academic, technical, or medical skills.
- **Teamster:** Adaptable workers, pilots, engineers, miners, and mechanics.

Classes mainly alter starting Stats, Saves, Wounds, skills, and Trauma Responses. They provide recognizable roles without long ability lists.

Skills are organized into prerequisite chains at Trained, Expert, and Master levels. They cover subjects such as firearms, piloting, hacking, engineering, medicine, robotics, exobiology, explosives, and command. Characters can therefore become mechanically specialized even though the basic rules remain simple.

Starting equipment is partly randomized, which helps establish a character quickly and ensures the crew may not have every tool it needs.

## Combat

Combat uses broad ranges rather than exact grid measurements: Adjacent, Close, Long, and Extreme.

Characters generally make Combat Checks to attack. Weapons have distinct damage, ammunition, range, and wound types. Armor has Armor Points; damage below the combined protection of armor and cover can be ignored, while a stronger hit destroys that protection and carries through.

When Health reaches zero, the character suffers a Wound, rolls on a wound table, and resets Health with any excess damage carried over. Wounds can represent bleeding, fractures, burns, gunshots, or severe trauma. Reaching maximum Wounds triggers a Death Save.

Combat is dangerous and unpredictable. Even characters trained for violence are vulnerable to concentrated fire, environmental hazards, and creatures that do not follow ordinary human limitations.

The system includes enough equipment and weapon detail to distinguish tactical approaches, but it does not assume balanced encounters or prolonged exchanges of attacks.

## Stress and Panic

Stress is the game’s defining mechanic.

Characters gain Stress through failed checks, failed saves, frightening situations, exhaustion, and other pressures. When a Panic Check is triggered, the player rolls a d20 and must exceed current Stress.

Failure produces a result from the Panic Table, ranging from temporary reactions to severe psychological or physical consequences. Each class also has a Trauma Response that can affect the rest of the crew.

Stress therefore creates a feedback loop: as a situation deteriorates, the crew becomes increasingly likely to make it worse.

## Character progression

Mothership does not use levels or conventional experience points.

During Shore Leave, characters may convert accumulated Stress into permanent improvements to their Saves. The quality and cost of the port determine how much Stress may be converted, and the process requires a Sanity Save.

Characters can also learn new skills through training, provided they have the time, money, and prerequisite knowledge. Equipment, cybernetic modification, contacts, ships, and accumulated resources can provide additional development.

Progress is concrete but usually slow. Survival itself is meaningful because long-lived characters gradually become more capable, better equipped, and more connected. A character can also die suddenly, so campaigns should not assume uninterrupted long-term advancement.

## Distinctive features

- Stress accumulates through failure and increases the risk of Panic.
- Each class has a Trauma Response that affects the wider crew.
- Skills form prerequisite trees with three degrees of mastery.
- Damage produces specific Wounds rather than only reducing a single hit-point total.
- Economic pressure, equipment, oxygen, ammunition, and medical care can all matter.
- Scenario design emphasizes surviving, solving, and saving rather than routine combat victory.
- The rules actively support mysteries and threats that cannot simply be killed.

## Practical considerations

Mothership is easy to begin playing and supports clearly differentiated technical and combat specialties. It works particularly well for contained scenarios, short campaigns, or episodic missions connected by a recurring crew and ship.

Its progression is less prominent than in a level-based game. Long campaigns depend heavily on access to training, Shore Leave, equipment, relationships, and economic pressure rather than regular power increases.

The system is explicitly designed for horror. Frequent combat, predictable victories, or characters who become broadly dominant can weaken the intended experience. Players must be comfortable with uncertainty, retreat, loss, and the possibility that survival is the main success condition.

Overall: Mothership is a focused science-fiction horror game with accessible rules, meaningful technical specialization, concrete enemy and equipment statistics, and strong pressure mechanics. It is best suited to campaigns where characters remain vulnerable and every mission may leave lasting consequences.
